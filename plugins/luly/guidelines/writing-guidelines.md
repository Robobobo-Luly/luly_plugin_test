# Luly Writing Guidelines

These rules keep lessons readable, consistent, and screen-friendly. All content-generating agents (intake, plan, fill, and any non-section-content agent) MUST follow them. The validator agent checks compliance before assemble.

## 1. Language & tone of voice
- Goal: explain complex ideas so even people who have no clue about crypto can understand. Reduce intimidation while staying accurate.
- Avoid overly technical phrasing, "docs-style" language, and inappropriate slang.
- Write in **American English**.
- Use everyday language — not simplified, not academic.
- **No emojis. No rhetorical questions. No long compound sentences.**

## 2. Titles & capitalization
- All lesson titles: **sentence case only** (not Title Case, not ALL CAPS).
- All story titles: **sentence case only**.

## 3. Sentence count per story
- Each story must be **3–5 sentences**. This is intentional.
- Stories must fit on one screen without scrolling or with very limited scrolling.
- A story cannot be only a bullet list. Bullets are allowed occasionally but cannot be the entire content.
- If there are bullets, they cant go straign after header, there should be opening sentence
- If a story needs more space, split it into two stories.

## 4. Periods & endings
- **No period** at the end of bullet points.
- **Headings never end with punctuation.**

## 5. Technical terms & pop-up explanations
- Technical terms are allowed when needed.
- When a term may be unfamiliar, use the pop-up functionality to explain it.
- Pop-up text should fit in one short sentence.

## 6. Quiz guidelines
- **One quiz per lesson, 2–3 questions per quiz.**
- Each question has **3 answer options; only one correct**.
- Questions must be answerable from the lesson content alone.
- Wrong answers must be clearly wrong — not partially correct.
- Avoid broad or vague questions that allow multiple interpretations.
- **Shuffle the position of the correct answer** across questions.

## 7. Course information
- **Course title** should not contain the name of the project. **3–4 words.**
- **Course description** is one short sentence capturing the course overview.
